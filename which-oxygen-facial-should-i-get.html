Which Oxygen Facial Is Right for Me?

Our Intraceuticals Oxygen Glow Facial comes in 8 variants — from $140 to $215. Here's how to choose, by skin concern, by occasion, by budget.

Pick by Concern See the Comparison

If this is your first Intraceuticals session and you don't have a specific concern in mind, start with Rejuvenate ($140). It's the base hydration infusion — the gentlest variant and the one most clients fall in love with on first try. You can layer targeted serums in future sessions once you know how your skin responds.

Each Variant, Explained

Rejuvenate — $140

The flagship infusion. Hyaluronic acid + vitamins delivered into the upper layers of skin via pressurized oxygen. Pure hydration and plumpness, no targeted active.

Best for: First-time Intraceuticals clients, sensitive skin, monthly maintenance, anyone who wants pure glow without addressing a specific concern. Pregnancy-safe.

Rejuve + Collagen — $150

Rejuvenate base + a layered collagen-supporting serum. Targets firmness, elasticity, and the visible effects of natural collagen loss.

Best for: Clients in their 40s and beyond, anyone noticing skin laxity, post-weight-loss clients, mature skin maintenance.

Rejuve + Vitamin C3 — $150

Rejuvenate base + a layered Vitamin C3 serum. C3 is a stabilized form of vitamin C that brightens, evens tone, and protects against free radical damage.

Best for: Dull or fatigued skin, after travel, post-illness recovery, anyone wanting an immediate brightening boost. Excellent before photos.

Rejuvenate + Antiox — $150

Rejuvenate base + a concentrated antioxidant serum. Targets environmental damage from UV, pollution, and oxidative stress.

Best for: Summer months in Tennessee sun, runners and cyclists who train outdoors, urban commuters, post-vacation recovery, anyone with cumulative sun damage.

Rejuvenate + Vitamin A — $150

Rejuvenate base + a retinol-adjacent Vitamin A serum. Vitamin A is the gold-standard anti-aging active — targets fine lines, texture, and uneven tone.

Best for: Anti-aging maintenance, clients who can't tolerate at-home retinol, mature skin. Not recommended during pregnancy or breastfeeding.

Clarity — $150

A targeted infusion designed for blemish-prone, oily, or congested skin. Contains salicylic acid and tea tree alongside the hydration base.

Best for: Active breakouts (mild), oily skin, congested pores, post-acne maintenance. Pairs well with our Acne Facial protocol for clients dealing with persistent breakouts.

Opulence Brightening — $160

A specialized brightening infusion targeting hyperpigmentation, melasma, sun spots, and uneven tone. Contains brightening botanicals plus licorice root extract.

Best for: Hyperpigmentation, melasma, post-acne dark spots, uneven tone. Most effective as a monthly series rather than one-off booking.

GlowTox — $215

Our premium variant. Contains peptides that temporarily relax muscle activity at the skin surface — producing a visible smoothing of expression lines (forehead, crow's feet, around the mouth) that lasts several days to a couple of weeks.

Best for: Pre-event prep (weddings, photoshoots, milestone birthdays), clients curious about Botox-like effects without injectables, dramatic same-day visible smoothing for a major occasion. Book 2–5 days before the event for peak effect.

Note: GlowTox is not a replacement for actual Botox if you want months of smoothing. It's the closest non-injectable equivalent for short-term, event-driven impact.

Pick By Scenario

"It's my first time getting an oxygen facial."

Choose: Rejuvenate ($140). Gentlest, most flattering, lowest commitment. Your esthetician will recommend a more targeted variant for next time based on what she sees.

"I'm getting married in the next month."

Pre-wedding series: Book a Rejuvenate or Rejuve + Vitamin C3 every 2–3 weeks in the lead-up. The week of the wedding: GlowTox ($215), 2–5 days before. The combination of hydration buildup plus the GlowTox event-day finish is unbeatable.

"My skin is dull and I have a big event in 5 days."

Choose: Rejuve + Vitamin C3 for brightness, or GlowTox if you want maximum impact and the budget supports it.

"I've got persistent breakouts."

Choose: Clarity ($150). Consider also booking the full Acne Facial ($250) protocol — it's a more comprehensive multi-step treatment for ongoing breakouts.

"I'm dealing with melasma from pregnancy or sun."

Choose: Opulence Brightening ($160), monthly for at least 3–4 months. Hyperpigmentation responds to consistent treatment more than to single sessions. Add Microderm in alternating months for additional surface work.

"I want to start an anti-aging routine."

Rotate: Rejuve + Collagen and Rejuve + Vitamin A every other month. Add Antiox during summer. Add GlowTox for special events.

"I'm pregnant and want an oxygen facial."

Choose: Rejuvenate ($140), Rejuve + Vitamin C3 ($150), or Opulence Brightening ($160). Avoid Rejuve + Vitamin A and GlowTox during pregnancy or breastfeeding. Tell us when you book.

"I just want the cheapest option that still feels like the real Intraceuticals experience."

Choose: Rejuvenate ($140). It's the foundation of every Intraceuticals session and many clients prefer it long-term because it's the most universally flattering.

Should You Add Enhancements?

Most of our Intraceuticals clients book the oxygen facial as a standalone. But certain enhancements pair beautifully:

  • Dermaplane (+$50) — performed before the Intraceuticals so the serums penetrate more evenly into freshly exfoliated skin.
  • Facial Cupping (+$40, +15 min) — depuffs and stimulates circulation. The combination of cupping + oxygen infusion is our favorite pre-event protocol.
  • Éminence Enzyme (+$35) — gentle pre-treatment exfoliation. Especially useful before Opulence or Clarity variants.
  • Light Therapy (+$30, +15 min) — collagen support and inflammation reduction. Particularly effective alongside Rejuve + Collagen or Rejuve + Vitamin A.

Talk with your esthetician at booking — she'll recommend the right combination based on what you're trying to achieve.

Frequently Asked Questions

What's the difference between all the Rejuvenate variants?

Rejuvenate is the base hydration infusion — hyaluronic acid plus vitamins. The "+" variants add a specific targeted serum on top of that hydration base. Collagen adds firming support; Vitamin C3 adds brightening antioxidants; Antiox adds environmental-damage protection; Vitamin A adds retinol-adjacent anti-aging. Same base, layered with one targeted concern.

Is GlowTox actually like Botox?

Not pharmacologically, but it produces a similar visible smoothing effect on expression lines through a different mechanism. GlowTox uses peptides that temporarily relax muscle activity at the surface — visible smoothing of forehead lines, crow's feet, and around the mouth that lasts several days to a couple of weeks. It's not a replacement for actual Botox if you want months of smoothing, but it's the closest non-injectable equivalent for a single special-event treatment.

Should I always start with Rejuvenate as a first-timer?

Yes, usually. Rejuvenate at $140 is the gentlest, most universally flattering variant — pure hydration and glow without any targeted active serum. It's a great way to see how your skin responds to the Intraceuticals system before committing to a higher-priced variant for a specific concern.

Can I get a different variant each time?

Absolutely. Many regulars rotate by season or by current concern — Rejuvenate for monthly maintenance, Antiox during summer, Vitamin C3 after travel, GlowTox before a major event. Your esthetician can recommend a rotation strategy based on your skin's needs over the year.

What if I have multiple concerns?

Pick the variant that matches your top concern. If you have multiple priorities — say, both pigmentation and aging — rotate between Opulence Brightening and Rejuve + Vitamin A across consecutive monthly visits rather than trying to stack them. Each variant is engineered to deliver its specific actives at optimal concentration.

Which variants are safe during pregnancy?

Rejuvenate, Rejuve + Collagen, Rejuve + Vitamin C3, Rejuve + Antiox, Clarity, and Opulence Brightening are generally pregnancy-safe. Avoid Rejuvenate + Vitamin A and GlowTox during pregnancy and breastfeeding — Vitamin A and the peptides in GlowTox are not recommended. Always confirm with your provider and tell us when you book.

How often should I get the oxygen facial?

Monthly is the typical cadence for maintenance. For active concerns (melasma, persistent dullness, anti-aging), every 2–3 weeks for a short series, then transition to monthly. For event prep, schedule strategically — 2–5 days before for GlowTox; 5–7 days before for other variants.
