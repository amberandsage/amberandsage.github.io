What "Organic Facial" Actually Means Here

The phrase gets thrown around. At Amber & Sage, it means something specific: we are a Proudly Partnered Eminence Organic Skin Care provider, and every facial in our menu uses Eminence products as the foundation. Eminence is a Hungarian-rooted, biodynamically farmed line — meaning the herbs and fruits in your serum were grown using moon-cycle and soil-health principles, without synthetic pesticides or fertilizers.

What you'll feel: products that are gentle enough for the most reactive skin yet potent enough to address real concerns. Free of parabens, sodium lauryl sulfates, propylene glycol, mineral oils, and animal testing.

Built around your skin, not a script

Every facial begins with a real consultation. Your esthetician will look at your skin under proper lighting, ask about your routine, your stressors, your meds, your hormones, your sleep — all the things that show up on the face. From there, she'll choose cleansers, exfoliants, masks, and finishing serums based on what your skin needs today, not a templated protocol.

This is the difference between an "organic facial" at a chain (same kit, every guest, every day) and a custom Eminence facial here: we build the treatment to you.

What to expect on the day

We're by appointment only, so it's recommended you show up about 5 minutes early. When it's time to come inside, we'll send you a text message — that way you can sit in your car, take a quiet moment, and decompress instead of waiting in a lobby. Once you come in, you'll be greeted with your choice of soothing tea or cold water, settled in a robe, and walked back to a private treatment room.

Our standard facial runs 60 minutes as a base. We also offer a 30-minute Express Facial — a refreshing treatment designed for a quick glow-up and deep reset. This service includes a double cleanse with steam, a thorough skin analysis, and the application of Éminence Organic Skin Care products customized to your unique skin needs. Perfect for a lunchtime pick-me-up or when you're short on time but still want radiant, nourished skin.

On the other end, enhancements can extend a session well past 90 minutes: facial cupping adds 15, light therapy adds 15, high frequency adds 5, and so on. A full custom 60-minute facial typically includes double cleansing, exfoliation, a steam or warm cloth, extractions only if needed (and only if you want them), masks tailored to your skin, lymphatic facial massage, and a finishing serum and SPF. You'll leave with a tiny list of recommendations if you'd like one — never a hard retail pitch.

Facials FAQ

What makes Eminence Organic Skin Care different?

Eminence is a Hungarian-rooted, biodynamic and organic skincare line — sustainably farmed, free of parabens, sodium lauryl sulfates, mineral oils, and other harsh chemicals. Their formulations are exceptionally gentle while still being clinically effective.

How is your facial customized to my skin?

Every facial begins with a skin analysis and consultation. Your esthetician selects cleansers, exfoliants, masks, and serums based on your skin's actual needs that day — not a fixed protocol.

Do I need dermaplaning or a peel?

Both are optional enhancements. Dermaplaning removes peach fuzz and surface dead skin for an instant smooth feel; enzyme peels address texture, pigmentation, and aging more deeply. Your esthetician will recommend what's appropriate.

Is this safe for sensitive or acne-prone skin?

Yes. Eminence is widely regarded as one of the gentlest professional lines on the market. Many of our clients came to us specifically because chain spas left their skin irritated or broken out.

How often should I get a facial?

Once a month is the most common cadence — it matches your skin's renewal cycle. For specific concerns like acne or hyperpigmentation, we may recommend a tighter rhythm for a few months, then ease off.

Can I bring my own products?

We use Eminence Organic Skin Care exclusively for our facials, so we can't substitute in products you bring from home. That said, if you have products you're already using and want guidance on — ingredient questions, whether they're working, how they layer with what we recommend — bring them along and your esthetician will happily talk through them with you.
