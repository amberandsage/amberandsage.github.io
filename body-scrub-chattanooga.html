What's Actually Happening

Body scrubs sound simple — sugar plus oil, rubbed onto skin. The difference between a forgettable scrub and a transformative one is in the ingredients, the technique, and the integration with the rest of the visit.

The product: Éminence Coconut Sugar Scrub

We use Éminence's organic Coconut Sugar Scrub — the same biodynamic brand we partner with for all our facial work. Raw sugar provides the gentle mechanical exfoliation; organic coconut oil delivers hydration and softness; tropical coconut scent leaves you faintly fragrant for hours. No synthetic fragrances, no harsh exfoliants, no plastic microbeads (banned in cosmetics for good reason).

The technique

Your therapist begins by warming the scrub before application — cold sugar on warm skin is uncomfortable; warm scrub feels like a coastal moment. The scrub is applied in sections (legs first, then arms, back, torso) and worked in with slow, soothing circular massage strokes that double as gentle lymphatic stimulation. The combination of exfoliation, massage, and oil absorption is what produces the visible glow.

The shower

After the scrub work is complete, you'll move to our shower to rinse. The sugar dissolves easily; the coconut oil leaves a light, nourishing film on the skin that doesn't need to be washed off aggressively. This is the moment most clients say feels best — warm water on freshly exfoliated, oil-conditioned skin.

What you'll notice

Immediately: skin is dramatically smoother to the touch. For 2–3 days afterward: lotion absorbs deeper and faster; everything feels softer; the faint coconut scent lingers; clothing slides on differently. For 5–7 days: the smoothness gradually settles back, but the skin holds moisturizer better than it did before the scrub.

Why "seasonal"?

We rotate our scrub menu through the year — coconut sugar is our current default, but seasonal variations may appear as the calendar shifts. The structure and benefits are consistent; the scent profile evolves with the seasons.

What to Expect

Plan to arrive about 5 minutes early

We're by appointment only, so plan to arrive 5 minutes early. We'll send you a text when it's time to come inside. You'll be greeted with your choice of soothing tea or cold water and walked to a private treatment room.

Undressing and draping

You'll undress to your comfort level (most clients prefer underwear off, but bottoms-on is welcome). Your therapist steps out while you change. Full draping is in place throughout — the area being worked on is exposed; everything else stays covered.

The scrub itself

About 20 minutes of warmed scrub application + soothing massage strokes across your back, arms, legs, and (with consent) torso. The pressure is gentle — this isn't deep tissue work, it's exfoliation with care.

The shower

You'll move to our shower (robe and slippers provided for the transit) and rinse for as long as you'd like — usually 5–10 minutes. We provide towels and a clean robe for after.

Walking out

Skin smoother than you remembered it being possible. Faint tropical coconut scent. A noticeable nervous-system reset. Most clients move slower for the rest of the day.

Frequently Asked Questions

What does the body scrub actually do?

It mechanically exfoliates the entire body — sloughing off dead skin cells from your back, shoulders, arms, legs, and torso using fine raw sugar suspended in nourishing oils. The exfoliation is followed by soothing massage strokes, then a warm shower. The result is skin that's noticeably smoother and softer, and that holds moisturizer and lotion dramatically better for days afterward.

Will the scrub be too rough on sensitive skin?

No — the sugar granules in Éminence's Coconut Sugar Scrub are fine and naturally dissolve as they're worked into the skin. The oils make the texture creamy rather than gritty. It's gentler than most salt-based scrubs and far gentler than mechanical body brushing.

Do I need to bring anything?

No. We provide robes, towels, and everything you'll need for the post-scrub shower. Just come clothed — you'll change in a private treatment room, and you'll have full draping throughout the service.

Should I shave or wax beforehand?

Avoid shaving or waxing the same day — freshly exfoliated skin is more sensitive to the sugar. Two to three days before is ideal if you want smooth, hair-free skin going into your scrub appointment.

Should I get a body scrub before or after a massage?

After is typically better. A massage often uses oil that you'd then want to rinse off; doing the scrub afterward removes the oil residue while adding its own nourishing layer. Then the post-scrub shower leaves you feeling completely refreshed at the end of the visit.

Is this safe during pregnancy?

Yes — sugar scrubs are generally pregnancy-safe. We use side-lying positioning instead of lying flat on the stomach if you're in your second or third trimester, and we'll skip any areas you're sensitive about. Tell us when you book.

How often should I get a body scrub?

Once a month is the most common cadence for regulars. For acute needs (pre-vacation, pre-wedding, between seasons), one-off bookings work well. There's no compounding "series" benefit the way there is with facials — each scrub is a standalone reset.

What if I'm allergic to coconut?

Tell us when you book — we may have alternative scrubs available depending on the season, or we may recommend skipping the service. Coconut allergies are rare but real, and we won't risk a reaction.
